Tallawah Farms on the Eastern Shore of Maryland is a group of growers native to the Caribbean. Black-owned and family-operated, their motto is "eat what you grow, and grow what you eat," and farming is very much their way of life.

Using sustainable practices on their 1.5-acre property, they specialize in ethnic crops from the Caribbean islands, including hot peppers, okra, and callaloo, plus cucumbers, sweet peppers, and more. 

Their name comes from a Jamaican term used to describe someone or something as strong, fearless, and not to be underestimated - and that captures the spirit and pride upheld by Tallawah Farms growers. 

Tallawah's red habanero peppers and lush callaloo greens have been spicing up our Produce Bags, and we love it! These unique veggies provide migrants with a taste of home while introducing something new and different to our community.
