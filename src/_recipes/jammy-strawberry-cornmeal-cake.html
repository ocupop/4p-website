--- layout: recipe title: Jammy Strawberry Cornmeal Cake author: 'Amanda Mac de Villiers, Grower Support Specialist at 4P Foods' description_html: >-

This sweet little cake celebrates strawberries and the start of summer. Using cornmeal in baking is a great Southern tradition and also adds toothsome texture! I like to use fruits and honey in my baking because I try to experiment with different ways to satisfy my sweet tooth while using less refined sugar.

ingredients_html: >-

Makes 1 round cake

  • 2 c fresh strawberries, halved
  • ½ c strawberry jam
  • 2 Tbsp raw turbinado sugar, for sprinkling
  • 1 ½ c AP flour
  • ¾ c cornmeal
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 stick or 8 Tbsp butter, melted
  • 2 Tbsp brown sugar
  • ½ c honey
  • ½ c buttermilk
  • zest of 1 lemon, ~1 Tbsp
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 eggs
directions_html: >-

Preheat oven to 350 F.

Grease & line a large round cake pan or liberally grease a 10” or 12” cast iron pan.

Cake: Whisk together dry ingredients. Combine melted butter, brown sugar, honey & buttermilk; beat till well mixed. Add lemon zest & vanilla. Beat in eggs one at a time.

Add half the dry ingredients and mix, then add in the second half and mix again.

Stir jam until it is a runny consistency (warm very lightly if needed to achieve runny consistency).

Pour batter into pan. Dollop jam in spots over the batter. Gently swirl the jam into the batter.

Press strawberry halves into the batter. I like to leave the strawberries uncovered by batter and alternate some cut side up and some cut side down.

Sprinkle all over with the 2 Tbsp raw turbinado sugar. Bake approximately 30 mins until golden.

Whip Cream: While the cake bakes, prepare fresh whipped cream: beat together 1 cup heavy cream, 1-2 Tbsp powdered sugar and 1 tsp vanilla extract for a few minutes, until stiff peaks form.

Let cake cool, then slice and served with whipped cream.
