We are your direct connection to the farm. Ordering a bag from us is like an online farmers market meets a farm share or CSA (Community Supported Agriculture) program. Choose between different kinds of bags, like produce, meat, or dairy. Order once or on a recurring basis. Each week, you'll receive our newsletter with that week's “menu” with a hand-selected range of seasonal items. (Customize by swapping out anything you don’t love for other things that you do). - question: Where does 4P source its food from? answer_html: >-

The short answer: we source locally and regionally from a network of over 200 small, independent farmers, most of them located in the Mid-Atlantic. Does that mean it’s “local?” That depends on where you live and how you define it, so we often use the term “regional” to describe our sourcing practices.

The longer answer: it depends on the item and the season. Avocados don’t grow in the Mid-Atlantic, so we buy them from a fair-trade cooperative in Peru (read more about our sourcing of tropical items on our blog here). As another example, we can offer peak-season strawberries longer by following their season up from the Carolinas in early spring through Virginia and beyond into mid-summer. 4P is a founding member of the Eastern Food Hub Collaborative, a network of other food hubs like us up and down the Eastern Seaboard who all work with their own vetted networks of small, sustainable farmers and work together to increase the variety and supply of produce available from this region.

Read our growing collection of farmer profiles for more information about our food.

- question: What if I want to skip a delivery? answer_html: >-

Go for it. On your account management page you have the option to skip your order once or for a longer period of time. All we ask is that you skip any upcoming deliveries ahead of the 2-day cut-off period, during which time we finalize orders with farmers. The cut-off begins at 11:59 pm on Sunday for a Wednesday delivery, 11:59 pm on Monday for a Thursday delivery, and 11:59 pm on Tuesday for a Friday delivery. 

- question: 'How do I skip an order, or multiple orders at once?' answer_html: >-

To skip once, login to your account and click on “My Account” in the upper right hand corner and then click on “Upcoming Deliveries.” You’ll see the orders you have scheduled for the next six weeks, including the items and total cost, and you’ll also have the option to skip or donate each individual order.

If you choose to skip, you won’t be charged for your order that week. If you choose to donate, you will be charged, and your order will be packed up and delivered to one of our food assistance partners, like the Fauquier Community Food Bank.

You can skip for a longer period of time by turning on a Vacation Hold. Under “My Account” click on “Delivery Hold” and enter your vacation dates - we will skip all deliveries scheduled that fall between the date you entered to leave and the date you entered to return. 

If you need any assistance skipping a delivery, please reach out to us and we'll be happy to help.

- question: How is 4P different than other online food delivery services? answer_html: >-

We deliver the freshest, highest-quality produce, meat, dairy, and pantry goods from farmers and producers in the Mid-Atlantic region (and sometimes beyond) who share our values of Good Food.

Our produce offerings vary with the seasons. In the Spring, you’ll see asparagus and strawberries. In the summer, tomatoes and sweet corn. Autumn equals apples and leafy greens. And in the Winter, it’s a mix of local greenhouse-grown produce like lettuce and storage crops like butternut squash, artisan goods from local makers, and fruits and vegetables from farmers further south. 

Year-round, we offer great local meats and dairy from animals raised on open pasture and treated with dignity throughout their lives, as well as delicious pantry goods like pastas, jams, and more made by local artisans. 

In short, we deliver the bounty of the regional foodshed, harvested daily and delivered to you. With 4P, you are voting with your fork for a more sustainable and equitable food system for everyone.

- question: Is all the food organic? What about non-GMO answer_html: >-

Some of it is USDA-certified organic, and some of it is not (not all farmers who use organic practices choose to get certified). While certifications are certainly important, what is most important to us is Good Food.

Good Food provides nourishment and enables people to thrive, is equitably accessible to everyone in the region, is produced in a manner that is environmentally regenerative, comes from a supply chain in which every being is treated with respect and dignity, and improves health outcomes and economic security for all.

We work with farmers who are good stewards of their resources, including land, water, and labor. Our farmer profiles are a great place to get more information about each farmer and their growing practices. While we embrace a local and regional food system, you can choose to customize your bags to include only local products, for example.

Our long-term goal is to be 100% supporting regenerative agriculture. Regenerative agriculture seeks not only to maintain but also to increase soil health, carbon sequestration, and biodiversity.

- question: How do I find out what food is in the bags this week? answer_html: >-

Each Saturday we update our online shop with what’s fresh that week and send a Weekly Harvest Newsletter straight to your inbox, making it simple to decide what you want to order and plan what you’ll cook for the week. We also include storage tips, recipe ideas, and highlights from our farmers and partners. 

If you’ve signed up and are not receiving the email newsletter, be sure to check your spam or promotions folder, and then please get in touch with us so we can look into it.

- question: Can I customize what I get in my bag? answer_html: >-

Absolutely. We took the best of a traditional CSA or farm share model (fresh, seasonal foods for you and direct, consistent support for farmers) and made it more flexible and customizable.

Simply log in and customize your bag. The time to shop from our fresh selection will be between midday Saturday (when the newsletter arrives) up until your skip deadline (two full days before the start of your delivery day, or for an example, Sunday at 11:59 pm for Wednesday delivery).

See what's available by either clicking on the box icon in the upper right hand corner or by navigating to Upcoming Deliveries under My Account, and click Customize Bag. From there, you can see what's on order, take out any items you don’t like, double up on your favorites, or add in something new. 

Be sure to click Save to complete your changes!

- question: Is there a minimum order or commitment? answer_html: >-

Our minimum order is $30, which comes out to $35 with the delivery fee. The minimum order amount to get free shipping is $60.

There is no minimum commitment and no tricky sign-up policy. You can order once to give us a try, or sign up for a subscription and still cancel anytime before your skip deadline with no fees–but if you were anything less than satisfied with your order, it might be worth getting in touch with our member experience team first so that we can try to make things right.

- question: Is there a delivery fee? answer_html: >-

Delivery is FREE for all orders over $50 and only a $5 flat fee for orders below $50.

In an effort to reduce our carbon footprint, we also offer free delivery to our public pick-up sites, where anyone can pick up their bags within a certain pick-up time frame!

- question: How does 4P address racial equity in the food system? answer_html: >-

Right off the bat, we want to acknowledge that the food system has traditionally done a lot of harm to BIPOC communities, from colonization to slavery to the poor treatment of immigrant farm laborers. We are intentionally working to increase our sourcing from BIPOC farmers and to center equity in our daily work as a company. You can read more about racial injustice in the food system and 4P’s response in this letter from our founder & CEO, Tom McDougall.

- question: What is my delivery day? answer_html: >-

You should have been notified of your delivery day during the signup process, but if you’re unsure, you can login into your account, click on “My Account” in the upper right hand corner, then click on “Upcoming Deliveries” to see all of your upcoming delivery dates. - question: How does delivery work? answer_html: >-

All of our deliveries are contactless, and you should receive an automatic email or text notification when a driver drops off your bags, but the specifics will depend on your delivery site.

In general, if you signed up for direct home delivery, we usually deliver the bags right to your doorstep, or wherever you indicated in your delivery instructions (if we don’t deliver to the right place, please get in touch to let us know!).

For commercial buildings or apartment complexes, we deliver the bags per the building’s COVID-19 protocols and your directions. Per our own COVID-19 protocols, all of our drivers wear required face masks and gloves--read more about our response to the pandemic here. If your building has limited open hours for deliveries as a result of COVID-19, please let us know so we can plan accordingly.

In an effort to maximize delivery efficiency and minimize contact during the busiest hours of the day, your delivery may arrive as early as 4:00AM and as late as 9:00PM. You will have the opportunity to tell us your delivery time preferences during registration, or you can reach out to us to let us know if you have timing limitations.

- question: What if I'm allergic to something? answer_html: >-

That’s where the customization function comes in! Be sure to login to your account between Saturday afternoon and your order deadline (two full days before your delivery day) to customize your order and swap out any items you can’t enjoy.

- question: Why can't I put meat in my produce bag? Or yogurt in my meat bag? answer_html: >-

We don’t yet have a safe way to pack meat (which comes frozen) with other items that aren’t frozen (or vis versa), without using lots of excess packaging, which we try to avoid from an environmental perspective. We are working to expand our a la carte capability, and in the meantime, our customizable bags and flexible order cadences (one-time, weekly, every other week, and so on) should help you get the right amounts of the right kinds of food for your household with minimal waste.

- question: How can I order extra items? answer_html: >-

After you choose your bag(s), head over to the shop to browse that week’s available items and add anything you would like. You can choose if you want this item just one time, or on a recurring basis (based on availability). If the item you choose does not show these options that means it has limited availability. 

Can’t find what you’re looking for? Try clicking the magnifying glass in the corner to search for a specific item.

- question: How is the food delivered? Will it stay fresh? answer_html: >-

You will receive an email or text notification when a driver drops off your bags, and as long as you can pick up your food the same day it is delivered, you should be fine. We deliver our produce in reusable insulated tote bags to keep everything fresh. For our meat, dairy, and eggs, we deliver them in nifty insulated cooler boxes to keep everything cold. We take all the packaging back to sanitize and reuse, so please set out your empties on delivery day for our driver to swap out.

- question: What should I do with the old bags? answer_html: >-

We carefully inspect, sanitize, and reuse them, so we would love to have them back! To help minimize both cost and waste, please return the old bags, cooler boxes (tote bag, cooler packs, and insulated box included), and any glass milk bottles to the same place where your bags were dropped off. We’ll send you a reminder email the day before your delivery so that our driver can pick them when they deliver the next day. We’ll then quarantine them and sanitize them for reuse. With your help, it’s a win-win! Thank you!

- question: How does billing work? answer_html: >-

We do our billing every Saturday for the prior week’s deliveries. So if you get a bag on Wednesday, you’ll be billed the following weekend. If you skip your order or add a vacation, you won’t be billed that week.

- question: What if I receive damaged produce or my delivery is missing an item? answer_html: >-

If you receive any disappointing veggies or sad fruit, or if any items are missing, please let us know! We’ll gladly try to correct whatever happened, whether through a refund or an extra item in your next delivery. 

We do our best to closely manage inventory so that we have enough for all orders but not so much that anything goes to waste, but if we ever run out of an item or it becomes unavailable after you have selected it in your order, we will do our best to notify you of the change so you can let us know what you’d like instead. If we can’t get in touch in time, we’ll substitute the closest item we have on hand (like pears for apples, or sweet potatoes for butternut squash).
